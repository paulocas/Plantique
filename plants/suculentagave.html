AGAVE

(Agave spp)

Agaves are a diverse genus of succulent plants known for their striking rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically rigid and often end in sharp points, giving many agave species a spiky appearance.

Some well-known varieties include Agave americana (Century Plant) with its large, architectural rosette and Agave parryi with its compact form.

Agaves are native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas, ranging from the southern United States to Central America. They are well-adapted to thrive in well-draining soils, often in rocky or desert environments.
