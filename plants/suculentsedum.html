SEDUM

(Sedum spp)

Sedum is a diverse genus of succulent plants that encompasses a wide range of shapes, sizes, and colors. Commonly known as stonecrops, these succulents are characterized by fleshy leaves and often form low, spreading mounds or ground covers.

Some popular varieties include Sedum spectabile, with its upright stems and pink flower heads, and Sedum spurium, known for its mat-forming habit.

Sedums are native to many parts of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America. They are well-adapted to various environments, from rocky mountain slopes to sandy coastal areas.
